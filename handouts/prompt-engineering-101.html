Prompt Engineering 101

How to Get the Best Results from an LLM

The Art of the Ask

Prompt engineering is the skill of crafting effective inputs (prompts) to guide a Large Language Model towards a desired output. It's like being a great director for a very talented but very literal actor. The clearer your instructions, the better the performance.

Core Techniques

1. Be Specific and Clear

Don't say: "Write about dogs." Say: "Write a 100-word paragraph about the loyalty of golden retrievers, in a warm and friendly tone."

2. Provide Context

Give the model the background information it needs. "I am a Year 12 student writing an essay on the Treaty of Waitangi. My main argument is..."

3. Assign a Persona

Tell the model who to be. "Act as a professional historian. Explain the causes of the New Zealand Wars."

4. Use Examples (Few-Shot Prompting)

Show the model what you want. "Translate the following from English to Te Reo Māori, like this: Hello -> Kia ora. Goodbye -> Haere rā. Thank you -> ?"
