Lesson 4.2: Tino Rangatiratanga - Defending Indigenous Sovereignty

Year 8 Social Studies: How Systems Shape Our Lives

Lesson Activities

1. Understanding Colonization (20 mins)

Explore how the Crown systematically violated Te Tiriti through land confiscations, language suppression, and institutional racism. Students create a timeline showing the gap between Te Tiriti promises and colonial reality.

2. Māori Political Action Study (30 mins)

Jigsaw study of three examples of Māori defending tino rangatiratanga: Te Whiti's peaceful resistance (1870s-80s), the 1975 Land March, and Bastion Point occupation. Focus on these as legitimate political actions, not "protests."

3. Decolonization Today (15 mins)

Examine contemporary examples: Māori Party, Ihumātao, He Puapua report. Students discuss how Māori continue to assert tino rangatiratanga through political systems.

4. Tauiwi Responsibilities (10 mins)

Reflection: "As tangata tiriti (people of the Treaty), what are our responsibilities to support tino rangatiratanga?" Connect to being good ancestors for future generations.

Learning Goal

I can explain how Māori have defended tino rangatiratanga against colonial systems and continue to do so today.

Cultural Protocol

Acknowledge that this learning involves painful histories. Support students processing colonization impacts.
