Lesson 4.1: Te Tiriti o Waitangi - Māori and Crown Governance Systems

Year 8 Social Studies: How Systems Shape Our Lives

Lesson Activities

1. Karakia & Māori Governance Systems (20 mins)

Begin with karakia. Explore traditional Māori governance through iwi, hapū, and whānau structures. Students map their own connections to place and people, understanding that Māori already had sophisticated political systems before 1840.

2. Te Tiriti: Two Governance Systems (25 mins)

Compare the Māori and English texts. Understand Kāwanatanga (governorship over settlers) vs Tino Rangatiratanga (absolute chieftainship/sovereignty). Students explore how Māori understood they were maintaining their own authority while allowing Crown governance over Pākehā.

3. Living Document Gallery Walk (20 mins)

Students rotate through stations showing how Te Tiriti remains a living document: Waitangi Tribunal, settlement processes, co-governance models, and contemporary Māori political institutions.

4. Whakapapa Connections (15 mins)

Students reflect: "How does understanding Te Tiriti as ongoing relationship change how we think about governance in Aotearoa today?" Connect to their own responsibility as tangata whenua or tauiwi.

Learning Goal

I can explain how Te Tiriti establishes two governance systems and remains a living relationship that shapes Aotearoa today.

Cultural Protocol

Begin with karakia to acknowledge mana whenua and create sacred learning space.
