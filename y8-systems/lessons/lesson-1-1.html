Lesson 1.1: What is a System?

Year 8 Social Studies: How Systems Shape Our Lives

Lesson Activities

1. Introduction & Brainstorm (10 mins)

Start by asking students what the word "System" means to them. Brainstorm ideas on the board (e.g., rules, order, computers, school, family). Explain that a system is a set of connected parts that form a whole, with rules, roles, and a purpose.

2. Interactive Sorting Activity (20 mins)

In small groups, students sort the "System Sorting Cards". They must place each card into an "Is a System" or "Is Not a System" pile and be able to justify their choices. Discuss tricky examples as a class.

3. Frayer Model: Defining "System" (15 mins)

Hand out the Frayer Model worksheet. As a class, or in pairs, students define the word "System", list its characteristics, and provide examples and non-examples based on the sorting activity.

4. Group Activity: School System Map (20 mins)

In groups, students create a 'system map' of the school on A3 paper. The map should show the different parts (people, places, rules) and how they are connected to achieve the school's purpose.

5. Exit Slip & Wrap-up (10 mins)

To finish, students answer an exit slip question: "What is one system you have used today, and what is its purpose?" This serves as a quick formative assessment.

Learning Goal

I can describe what a system is and why we need them.
