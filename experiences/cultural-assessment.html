🏆 Cultural Assessment Strategies - Aromatawai

Authentic evaluation honoring diverse ways of demonstrating knowledge and understanding

Whakataukī: "Mā te huruhuru ka rere ai te manu"
Feathers enable the bird to fly - Success comes from preparation and diverse skills
📁 Portfolio Assessment - Kete Mātauranga

Living Portfolio of Learning

Cultural Foundation: Like a traditional kete (basket) that holds precious taonga, students curate their learning artifacts to tell their educational journey story.

Portfolio Components:

📝 Written Reflections
🎥 Video Presentations
🎨 Creative Projects
📊 Research Evidence
🗣️ Oral Narratives
🤝 Peer Feedback

Cultural Assessment Criteria:

Taumata 4
Demonstrates deep cultural understanding with sophisticated connections between traditional and contemporary knowledge
Taumata 3
Shows clear cultural awareness and makes meaningful connections to personal and community contexts
Taumata 2
Demonstrates developing cultural knowledge with some personal connections and understanding
Taumata 1
Shows beginning awareness of cultural concepts with support and scaffolding
🎭 Community Presentation - Whakakitenga

Sharing Knowledge with Whānau

Cultural Foundation: Following traditional hui protocols, students present their learning to community members, elders, and whānau, receiving feedback from those who will benefit from their knowledge.

Presentation Formats:

  • Whakatōhea Style: Formal presentation with cultural protocols
  • Kōrero Taiao: Environmental storytelling with local examples
  • Digital Hui: Online presentation connecting multiple communities
  • Waiata Integration: Learning shared through song and performance
Assessment Focus: How effectively does the student communicate complex ideas to diverse community audiences while maintaining cultural respect and accuracy?
👥 Peer Teaching - Ako-ā-Teina

Learning Through Teaching Others

Cultural Foundation: The tuakana-teina relationship where older students support younger learners reflects traditional Māori educational structures and demonstrates deep understanding.

Peer Teaching Opportunities:

  • Cross-Year Mentoring: Year 13 students teaching Year 9s
  • Cultural Knowledge Sharing: Students teaching Te Reo Māori to parents
  • Skill Workshops: Demonstrating practical learning to peers
  • Community Education: Teaching environmental concepts to local groups

Teaching Effectiveness Criteria:

Excellent
Adapts teaching methods to different learning styles, shows cultural sensitivity, demonstrates deep subject mastery
Good
Communicates clearly, engages learners effectively, shows solid understanding of content and cultural context
🏗️ Community Project - Kaupapa Hapori

Real-World Impact Assessment

Cultural Foundation: Students undertake projects that benefit their community, following the principle of manaakitanga (caring for others) and creating lasting positive impact.

Project Examples:

  • Environmental Restoration: Native plantings with cultural protocols
  • Cultural Documentation: Recording elder stories and knowledge
  • Community Gardens: Sustainable food production using Māori methods
  • Digital Archives: Preserving local history and language
Assessment Focus: Projects evaluated on cultural authenticity, community benefit, collaborative skills, and integration of academic learning with practical application.
🌿 Holistic Reflection - Whakaaro Tapatahi

Integrated Learning Reflection

Cultural Foundation: Drawing from traditional practices of whakaaro (reflection) and whakatōhea (contemplation), students explore how learning connects to their personal, cultural, and spiritual development.

Reflection Components:

  • Personal Growth: How has learning changed your perspective?
  • Cultural Connections: Links between learning and cultural identity
  • Community Relevance: Applications to whānau and hapū needs
  • Future Pathways: How learning guides future choices

Reflection Formats:

📔 Written Journals
🎤 Audio Reflections
🎭 Performance Pieces
🎨 Visual Narratives
💬 Discussion Circles
🌱 Growth Portfolios
⭐ Competency Demonstration - Whakakite Pukenga

Skills-Based Evidence Collection

Cultural Foundation: Like traditional assessments where rangatahi demonstrated readiness for adult responsibilities, students provide evidence of competency through multiple contexts and applications.

Competency Areas:

  • Cultural Competence: Understanding and applying cultural protocols
  • Communication: Effective expression in multiple contexts and languages
  • Critical Thinking: Analysis and evaluation using diverse knowledge systems
  • Collaboration: Working effectively within cultural and contemporary frameworks
  • Innovation: Creative problem-solving honoring traditional and modern approaches

Evidence Collection Methods:

Observed
Teacher/community member observes competency demonstration in authentic contexts
Documented
Student provides portfolio evidence showing competency development over time
Applied
Competency demonstrated through real-world application benefiting community

🌟 Assessment Philosophy - Kaupapa Aromatawai

Authentic Assessment honors diverse ways of knowing and being. These strategies recognize that ākonga demonstrate understanding through multiple pathways, honoring both traditional Māori approaches to learning evaluation and contemporary educational excellence.

Every assessment is an opportunity for growth, connection, and celebration of learning achievements.
