Unit Project: The Persuasive Campaign

A Summative, Project-Based Assessment

The Big Idea: What is Your Cause?

Throughout this unit, we have explored how language and visual texts can be used to persuade an audience. Now, it's your turn to put these skills into action. Your task is to create a complete persuasive campaign for a social or environmental issue that you are passionate about. You will work in a small group to research your chosen issue, develop a clear message, and create a set of persuasive texts designed to convince your audience to think, feel, or act differently.

Campaign Components

Your final campaign must include the following three components:

1. The Speech

You will write and perform a 2-3 minute persuasive speech that forms the core of your campaign's argument. This speech must be well-structured (using PEEL) and employ rhetorical devices (Ethos, Pathos, Logos) to be effective.

2. The Visual Text

You will create a visual component to support your campaign. This could be a poster, a political cartoon, or a short (30-60 second) video advertisement. It must use visual language techniques to reinforce your message.

3. The Written Rationale

You will submit a 300-400 word written rationale that explains the choices you made in your campaign. You must analyse your own work, explaining how you used specific persuasive techniques (both written and visual) to target your chosen audience.

Assessment Criteria

Your campaign will be assessed on the following criteria:

  • Clarity and Impact of Argument: Is your campaign's main message clear, focused, and compelling?
  • Use of Persuasive Techniques: Have you effectively used a range of written and visual techniques to persuade your audience?
  • Research and Evidence: Is your argument supported by credible research and evidence?
  • Creativity and Presentation: Is your campaign engaging, polished, and well-presented?
  • Analysis and Rationale: Does your rationale clearly explain the persuasive choices you made?
