Year 8 Social Studies Unit

How Systems Shape Our Lives

A comprehensive 5-week unit for Year 8 Social Studies, exploring government, rights, and civic action. This unit is being updated with a stronger Te Ao MƒÅori perspective.

Week 1: Systems, Rights & Responsibilities

Lesson 1.1: What is a System?

An introduction to systems thinking, identifying systems in our everyday lives.

Lesson 1.2: Rights & Responsibilities

Exploring the balance between individual rights and our responsibilities to the community.

Week 2: Types of Government

Lesson 2.1: What Kinds of Governments Exist?

Comparing different systems of government from around the world.

Lesson 2.2: Who Has Power?

An introduction to the concept of power and how it is distributed in different systems.

Week 3: Local Government & Community Voice

Lesson 3.1: How Does Local Government Work?

Investigating the role of local councils and how their decisions impact our lives.

Lesson 3.2: Who Gets a Say?

Exploring the ways that citizens can participate in local democracy and make their voices heard.

Week 4: The Treaty & Challenging Unfairness

Lesson 4.1: What is Te Tiriti o Waitangi?

An introduction to the Treaty of Waitangi as a foundational document of Aotearoa.

Lesson 4.2: Has the System Always Been Fair?

Examining historical and contemporary examples of how systems can create injustice.

Week 5: Design Your Own System

Lesson 5.1: Plan Your Own System

A project-based lesson where students design a fair and equitable system for a real-world scenario.

Lesson 5.2: Present and Reflect

Students present their system designs and reflect on the challenges of creating a fair system.
