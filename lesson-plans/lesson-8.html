Lesson 8: Peer Feedback & Rehearsal

Refining work through constructive criticism.

Learning Intentions (Ngā Whāinga Ako)

  • To provide constructive and specific feedback (kōrero āwhina) to peers using a structured model.
  • To receive feedback gracefully and use it to improve one's own work.
  • To refine the delivery of a persuasive speech and the clarity of a visual text.

NZC Links (Level 5)

  • **Listening, Reading, and Viewing:** "Analyse and evaluate how verbal and non-verbal language is used to create meaning and effect."
  • **Speaking, Writing, and Presenting:** "Refine oral text, using feedback and self-assessment."

Cultural Competencies (Tātaiako)

  • Manaakitanga: Showing care and respect when giving and receiving feedback, ensuring a safe and supportive learning environment.
  • Wānanga: Engaging in productive dialogue that is focused on improving student work and understanding.

Resources

Student drafts of their campaign speeches and visual texts.

Lesson Activities (75 Minutes)

1. Whakatau / Do Now: What is Kōrero Āwhina? (10 mins)

In pairs, students create a T-chart. On one side, they list characteristics of "Helpful Feedback" (e.g., specific, kind, actionable). On the other, they list "Unhelpful Feedback" (e.g., vague, mean, "It's good."). Co-construct class criteria for giving kōrero āwhina (constructive feedback).

2. Explicit Teaching: The "Two Stars and a Wish" Model (15 mins)

Teacher introduces a structured model for giving feedback that aligns with the class criteria: Two Stars and a Wish (He Kōrero Whetū e Rua, He Kōrero Wawata).

  • Star 1 (Whetū 1): Identify one specific thing that was done well. (e.g., "I really liked your strong eye contact during the speech.")
  • Star 2 (Whetū 2): Identify another specific thing that was effective. (e.g., "Your use of the statistic about obesity was a powerful piece of evidence.")
  • Wish (Wawata): Suggest one specific area for improvement, framed as a wish. (e.g., "I wish you would slow down your pace a little so we can absorb all your great points.")

Model the process by giving feedback on a student example (with permission) or the "Empty Playground" speech.

3. Project Work: Peer Feedback Rotations (45 mins)

This is a structured workshop. Groups are paired up.

  • Rotation 1 (20 mins): Group A presents their campaign speech and visual to Group B. Group B listens and then provides verbal feedback using the "Two Stars and a Wish" model. Group A takes notes.
  • Rotation 2 (20 mins): The roles are reversed. Group B presents and Group A provides feedback.
  • Reflection (5 mins): Groups return to their own space and discuss the feedback they received. They create a short action plan of 2-3 key changes they will make.

Differentiation:

  • Support: Provide feedback sentence starters for each part of the model (e.g., "One thing I really liked was...", "A second strength was...", "For next time, I wish you would consider...").
  • Extension: Challenge students giving feedback to also comment on the connection between the speech and the visual text. Is it a cohesive campaign?

4. Whakakapi / Exit Ticket (5 mins)

Each student writes down the single most helpful piece of kōrero āwhina they received and one action they will take because of it.
