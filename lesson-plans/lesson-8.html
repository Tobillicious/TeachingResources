Lesson 8: Peer Feedback & Rehearsal

Refining work through constructive criticism.

Learning Intentions

  • To provide constructive and specific feedback to peers using a structured model.
  • To receive feedback gracefully and use it to improve one's own work.
  • To refine the delivery of a persuasive speech and the clarity of a visual text.

NZC Links (Level 5)

  • **Listening, Reading, and Viewing:** "Analyse and evaluate how verbal and non-verbal language is used to create meaning and effect."
  • **Speaking, Writing, and Presenting:** "Refine oral text, using feedback and self-assessment."

Resources

Student drafts of their campaign speeches and visual texts.

Lesson Activities (75 Minutes)

1. Do Now: What Makes Good Feedback? (10 mins)

In pairs, students create a T-chart. On one side, they list characteristics of "Helpful Feedback" (e.g., specific, kind). On the other, they list "Unhelpful Feedback" (e.g., vague, mean, "It's good."). Discuss as a class.

2. Explicit Teaching: The "Two Stars and a Wish" Model (15 mins)

Teacher introduces a structured model for giving feedback: Two Stars and a Wish.

  • Star 1: Identify one specific thing that was done well. (e.g., "I really liked your strong eye contact during the speech.")
  • Star 2: Identify another specific thing that was effective. (e.g., "Your use of the statistic about obesity was a powerful piece of evidence.")
  • Wish: Suggest one specific area for improvement, framed as a wish. (e.g., "I wish you would slow down your pace a little so we can absorb all your great points.")

Model the process by giving feedback on a student example (with permission) or the "Empty Playground" speech.

3. Project Work: Peer Feedback Rotations (45 mins)

This is a structured workshop. Groups are paired up.

  • Rotation 1 (20 mins): Group A presents their campaign speech and visual to Group B. Group B listens and then provides verbal feedback using the "Two Stars and a Wish" model. Group A takes notes.
  • Rotation 2 (20 mins): The roles are reversed. Group B presents and Group A provides feedback.
  • Reflection (5 mins): Groups return to their own space and discuss the feedback they received. They create a short action plan of 2-3 key changes they will make based on the feedback.

4. Plenary / Exit Ticket (5 mins)

Each student writes down the single most helpful piece of feedback they received during the session.
