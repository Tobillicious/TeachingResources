Lesson 7: Visual Campaign Workshop

Creating the visual component of the persuasive campaign.

Learning Intentions

  • To apply knowledge of visual language techniques to create a persuasive visual text.
  • To work collaboratively to produce a visual component that aligns with the campaign's overall message.
  • To manage time effectively to complete a key component of the project.

NZC Links (Level 5)

  • **Speaking, Writing, and Presenting:** "Combine verbal and visual features to create meaning and effect, and justify the choices made."
  • **Visual Arts:** "Develop and clarify ideas, using a range of conventions from established practice."

Resources

Political Cartoon Analysis Handout Film Scene Analysis Handout

Plus art supplies (paper, markers, coloured pencils) or digital tools (Canva, Adobe Express, video editing software).

Lesson Activities (75 Minutes)

1. Do Now: Visual Hook (5 mins)

Show students a powerful and well-known poster or advertisement (e.g., the "We Can Do It!" poster, a famous brand ad). In one sentence, students describe the main message the visual is trying to send.

2. Explicit Teaching: Review & Goal Setting (10 mins)

Briefly review the key visual techniques (Symbolism, Caricature, Colour, Camera Angles, etc.). Remind students that the goal of their visual is to support and enhance the argument made in their speech.

Teacher sets a clear goal for the lesson: "By the end of this lesson, every group must have a completed first draft of their visual component."

3. Project Work: Visual Creation Workshop (55 mins)

This is a dedicated work session. Students get into their campaign project groups and work on creating their visual text.

  • Groups work from their plans and storyboards from Lesson 4.
  • Teacher circulates, acting as a facilitator and providing feedback. Key questions to ask groups:
    • "How does this visual connect to your main message?"
    • "What symbol have you used and why?"
    • "How does your choice of colour affect the mood?"
  • Ensure groups are managing their time effectively and that all members are contributing.

4. Plenary / Exit Ticket (5 mins)

Each group places their visual draft on their desk. Students do a silent "gallery walk" around the room to see the work of other groups. They are given one sticky note to leave a piece of positive feedback on another group's work.
