Lesson 4: Deconstructing Visual Texts

Analysing Political Cartoons and other Visual Media.

Learning Intentions

  • To identify key techniques used in political cartoons (Symbolism, Caricature, Labelling, Irony).
  • To analyse how these techniques are used to convey a persuasive message.
  • To begin planning the visual component of the campaign project.

NZC Links (Level 5)

  • **Listening, Reading, and Viewing:** "Show an increasing understanding of how the combination of verbal and visual features communicates ideas and information."
  • **Speaking, Writing, and Presenting:** "Combine verbal and visual features to create meaning and effect."

Resources

Political Cartoon Analysis Handout

Lesson Activities (75 Minutes)

1. Do Now: Visual Brainstorm (5 mins)

Show students a simple, common symbol (e.g., a skull and crossbones, a recycling logo). In their books, they write down what the symbol means and what ideas or feelings it evokes.

2. Explicit Teaching: The Language of Cartoons (20 mins)

Teacher introduces political cartoons as a form of persuasive visual text.

  • Distribute the Political Cartoon Analysis handout.
  • Discuss the four key techniques (Symbolism, Caricature, Labelling, Irony), using the examples on the sheet.
  • As a class, work through the deconstruction and analysis tasks (Questions 1 & 2) on the handout.

3. Project Work: Planning the Visual Campaign (45 mins)

Students get into their campaign project groups.

  • Task 1 (20 mins): Groups complete the application task (Question 3) on the handout, sketching a simple cartoon for their own campaign topic. This is a low-stakes planning activity.
  • Task 2 (25 mins): Groups decide on the format for their final visual text (e.g., poster, cartoon, short video). They will create a storyboard or a rough sketch of their visual, identifying the key symbols, colours, and text they plan to use to convey their message.

4. Plenary / Exit Ticket (5 mins)

One person from each group does a "gallery walk" to look at the other groups' visual plans. They return to their group and share one interesting idea they saw.
