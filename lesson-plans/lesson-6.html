Lesson 6: Speech Writing & Rehearsal

A practical workshop for drafting and delivering persuasive speeches.

Learning Intentions

  • To draft a full persuasive speech using the PEEL structure and rhetorical devices.
  • To understand the importance of delivery (tone, pace, body language) in a speech.
  • To practise and refine the delivery of a speech with peer feedback.

NZC Links (Level 5)

  • **Speaking, Writing, and Presenting:** "Deliver a fluent and coherent oral text that is organised and structured for a particular purpose."
  • **Speaking, Writing, and Presenting:** "Use verbal and non-verbal language to create an impact on an audience."

Lesson Activities (75 Minutes)

1. Do Now: Powerful Openings (5 mins)

Play the first 30 seconds of a famous, powerful speech (e.g., Martin Luther King Jr.'s "I Have a Dream"). Students write down what makes the opening so effective.

2. Explicit Teaching: The Importance of Delivery (15 mins)

Teacher explains that a great speech is more than just words on a page.

  • Discuss key elements of delivery: Pace (speed), Pitch (high/low), Power (volume), Pause, and Body Language.
  • Model reading a sentence from the "Empty Playground" speech in two different ways (e.g., monotone vs. passionate) to demonstrate the impact of delivery.

3. Project Work: Speech Drafting & Rehearsal (50 mins)

Students get into their campaign project groups.

  • Task 1 (30 mins): Groups work together to write the full draft of their 2-3 minute campaign speech. They must incorporate their PEEL paragraphs, rhetorical devices, a strong hook, and a powerful conclusion.
  • Task 2 (20 mins): In their groups, students begin rehearsing their speeches. Each member should have a speaking role. The group should provide feedback to each other on both the content and the delivery.

4. Plenary / Exit Ticket (5 mins)

Each student writes down one specific goal for their own delivery (e.g., "I will make more eye contact," "I will speak slower and use pauses").
