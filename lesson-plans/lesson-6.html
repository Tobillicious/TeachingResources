Lesson 6: Speech Writing & Rehearsal

A practical workshop for drafting and delivering persuasive speeches.

Learning Intentions (Ngā Whāinga Ako)

  • To draft a full persuasive speech (whaikōrero) using a clear structure and rhetorical devices.
  • To understand the importance of delivery (tone, pace, body language) in a speech.
  • To practise and refine the delivery of a speech with peer feedback.

NZC Links (Level 5)

  • **Speaking, Writing, and Presenting:** "Deliver a fluent and coherent oral text that is organised and structured for a particular purpose."
  • **Speaking, Writing, and Presenting:** "Use verbal and non-verbal language to create an impact on an audience."

Cultural Competencies (Tātaiako)

  • Whanaungatanga: Working collaboratively and building on each other's ideas to draft a cohesive group speech.
  • Ako: Understanding that effective delivery can be learned through practice and feedback from peers.

Lesson Activities (75 Minutes)

1. Whakatau / Do Now: Powerful Openings (10 mins)

Play the first 30 seconds of a famous, powerful speech (e.g., Whina Cooper's 1975 Te Matakite speech, Martin Luther King Jr.'s "I Have a Dream"). Students write down what makes the opening so effective, considering both the words and the delivery.

2. Explicit Teaching: The 5 P's of Delivery (15 mins)

Teacher explains that a great speech is more than just words on a page; it has powerful delivery.

  • Introduce the 5 P's of Delivery: Pace (speed), Pitch (high/low), Power (volume), Pause (deliberate silence), and Posture (body language).
  • Model reading a sentence from the "Empty Playground" speech in two different ways (e.g., monotone vs. passionate) to demonstrate the impact of the 5 P's.

3. Project Work: Whaikōrero Drafting & Rehearsal (45 mins)

Students get into their campaign project groups.

  • Task 1 (25 mins): Groups work together to write the full draft of their 2-3 minute campaign speech (whaikōrero). They must incorporate their PEEL paragraphs, rhetorical devices, a strong hook, and a powerful conclusion.
  • Task 2 (20 mins): In their groups, students begin rehearsing their speeches. Each member should have a speaking role. The group should provide feedback to each other on both the content and the 5 P's of delivery.

Differentiation:

  • Support: Provide cue cards with the 5 P's for observers to use when giving feedback. Provide a clear structure template for the speech.
  • Extension: Challenge students to record their rehearsal on a device and self-assess their delivery, setting specific goals for improvement.

4. Whakakapi / Exit Ticket (5 mins)

Each student writes down one specific goal for their own delivery based on the 5 P's (e.g., "I will use pauses after my key points," "I will stand with a more confident posture").
