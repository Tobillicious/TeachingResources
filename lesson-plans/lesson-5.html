Lesson 5: Research & Media Literacy

Finding and Evaluating Credible Evidence.

Learning Intentions

  • To understand the difference between a credible source and a biased or unreliable one.
  • To identify strategies for verifying information (e.g., checking the source, corroboration).
  • To begin gathering relevant evidence for the campaign project.

NZC Links (Level 5)

  • **Listening, Reading, and Viewing:** "Critically evaluate the reliability and usefulness of texts with increasing confidence."
  • **Social Sciences:** "Understand how people seek, use, and participate in social action."

Resources

Media Literacy Handout Cognitive Biases Handout

Lesson Activities (75 Minutes)

1. Do Now: Fact or Fiction? (10 mins)

Present students with three "facts," two of which are subtly false or misleading (e.g., "A study proved chocolate cures the common cold," "All sharks are dangerous to humans"). Students vote on which one they think is true. Discuss why it can be hard to tell the difference.

2. Explicit Teaching: The Challenge of 'Fake News' (20 mins)

Teacher introduces the concepts of misinformation, disinformation, and cognitive biases.

  • Use the Media Literacy Handout to discuss the difference between misinformation and disinformation, and the concept of "filter bubbles."
  • Introduce Confirmation Bias and the Availability Heuristic using the Cognitive Biases Handout.
  • Model how to evaluate a source by asking: Who made it? Why? Who benefits?

3. Project Work: Research & Evidence Gathering (40 mins)

Students get into their campaign project groups.

  • Task 1 (25 mins): Groups begin conducting research for their campaign topic. They must find at least three credible sources (e.g., reputable news articles, government websites, scientific studies) that provide evidence to support their arguments.
  • Task 2 (15 mins): For each source, students must write a one-sentence summary of the key piece of evidence they plan to use in their speech. They should also note down the source details for referencing.

4. Plenary / Exit Ticket (5 mins)

Each group shares one piece of evidence they found and the source it came from. As a class, briefly discuss the credibility of the source.
