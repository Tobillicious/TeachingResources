Lesson 9: Rationale & Final Polish

Explaining your choices and preparing for presentation.

Learning Intentions

  • To understand the purpose and structure of a written rationale.
  • To analyse and explain the persuasive choices made in their own work.
  • To complete a final review and polish of all campaign components.

NZC Links (Level 5)

  • **Speaking, Writing, and Presenting:** "Justify the choices made, in terms of the combination of features used to create meaning and effect."
  • **Thinking:** "Analyse and critique the reasoning of others." (Metacognitive skill applied to own work).

Resources

Unit Project Brief

Lesson Activities (75 Minutes)

1. Do Now: Why Did You Choose...? (5 mins)

Students answer a quick question in their books: "Think about the visual you created last lesson. Why did you choose that specific main image or symbol?"

2. Explicit Teaching: What is a Rationale? (15 mins)

Teacher explains that a rationale is a piece of writing that explains the reasoning behind your choices. It's the "why" behind the "what."

Introduce a simple structure for the rationale:

  • Introduction: Briefly state your campaign's overall goal and target audience.
  • Body Paragraph 1 (Speech): Explain your key persuasive choices. (e.g., "We used pathos in our introduction by... because we wanted the audience to feel...").
  • Body Paragraph 2 (Visual): Explain your key visual choices. (e.g., "We used the colour red as a symbol for... to create a sense of...").
  • Conclusion: Briefly summarise how you believe your choices will help you achieve your campaign's goal.

3. Project Work: Final Polish (50 mins)

This is the final dedicated work session before presentations.

  • Task 1 (25 mins): In groups, students write the first draft of their 300-400 word rationale.
  • Task 2 (25 mins): Groups use the remaining time to conduct a final review of all three campaign components. They should rehearse their speech one last time, check their visual for any errors, and proofread their rationale.

4. Plenary / Exit Ticket (5 mins)

On a sticky note, each student writes down one thing they are proud of about their group's campaign and one thing they are nervous about for the presentation.
