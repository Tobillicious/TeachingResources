Lesson: Sustainable Technology Design Challenge

Innovating for a Better Future, Inspired by the Past

Learning Objectives

  • Ākonga will be able to identify a local environmental issue.
  • Ākonga will be able to design a technological solution that integrates principles of kaitiakitanga.
  • Ākonga will be able to explain how their design is inspired by both mātauranga Māori and modern science.

Materials

  • Copies of the "Sustainable Technology Design Challenge" handout
  • Whiteboard or large sheets of paper
  • Markers, pens, and pencils
  • Optional: Recycled materials for building models (cardboard, plastic bottles, etc.)

Lesson Sequence

1. Introduction (10 mins)

Begin with a discussion about sustainability and kaitiakitanga. What do these terms mean? How do they relate to technology?

2. The Challenge (15 mins)

Introduce the design challenge. Go through the different options and allow students to choose a challenge that interests them. Encourage them to form groups based on their chosen challenge.

3. Design and Develop (30 mins)

In their groups, students work through the "Design Canvas" on the handout. They should research, brainstorm, and sketch their ideas. The teacher circulates to provide guidance and ask probing questions.

4. Presentations (15 mins)

Each group presents their design to the class. They should explain the problem, their solution, and how it connects to both mātauranga Māori and modern science.

Assessment for Learning

Use the group presentations as a key assessment point. Are students able to clearly articulate the connection between their design and the principles of kaitiakitanga? Does their design address the identified environmental issue? The completed "Design Canvas" can also be collected as evidence of learning.

Differentiation

For Support: Provide a more structured design brief with specific constraints and requirements. Work with a small group to help them develop their ideas.

For Extension: Challenge students to create a business plan for their sustainable technology. Who would be the target audience? How would it be funded?
