Lesson: The Design Thinking Process

A human-centred approach to solving problems creatively and collaboratively.

Learning Objectives

  • Ākonga will be able to define the five stages of the Design Thinking process.
  • Ākonga will be able to apply the Design Thinking process to a simple design challenge.
  • Ākonga will be able to work collaboratively to brainstorm and develop ideas.

Materials

  • Copies of the "Design Challenge: The Perfect School Bag" handout (printable version of the original handout)
  • Whiteboard or large sheets of paper
  • Markers, pens, and pencils
  • Sticky notes

Lesson Sequence

1. Introduction (10 mins)

Introduce the concept of "Design Thinking" as a way to solve problems by focusing on people. Ask students about problems they face with everyday objects.

2. Exploring the Stages (15 mins)

Go through the five stages of Design Thinking (Empathise, Define, Ideate, Prototype, Test) using the visual guide. For each stage, ask students for examples.

3. Design Challenge (25 mins)

In small groups, students work through the "Perfect School Bag" design challenge on the handout. Encourage them to spend time on each stage.

4. Sharing and Feedback (10 mins)

Each group shares their prototype sketch and one key idea. The class provides constructive feedback.

Assessment for Learning

Observe students' participation in group discussions. Review their completed "Design Challenge" handouts to check for understanding of the process. The quality of their prototype sketch and their ability to explain their design choices will demonstrate their grasp of the concepts.

Differentiation

For Support: Provide sentence starters for the "Define" stage. Work with a small group to guide them through the process.

For Extension: Challenge students to create a more detailed prototype using craft materials, or to interview other students to gather more authentic "Empathy" data.
