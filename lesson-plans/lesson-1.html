Lesson 1: Introduction to Persuasion

Unpacking the unit project and the fundamentals of persuasive language.

Learning Intentions

  • To understand the goals and assessment requirements of the "Persuasive Campaign" unit project.
  • To define the three main purposes of writing (PIE: Persuade, Inform, Entertain).
  • To identify the author's purpose in a range of short texts.

NZC Links (Level 5)

  • **Listening, Reading, and Viewing:** "Show an increasing understanding of how texts are shaped for different purposes and audiences."
  • **Speaking, Writing, and Presenting:** "Select, develop, and communicate ideas on a range of topics."

Resources

Unit Project Brief Author's Purpose (PIE) Handout

Lesson Activities (75 Minutes)

1. Do Now: What is Persuasion? (5 mins)

In pairs, students brainstorm a definition for "persuasion" and list as many examples of persuasive texts as they can think of (e.g., ads, movie trailers, political speeches, opinion articles).

2. Explicit Teaching: Unpacking the Unit (20 mins)

Teacher introduces the "Critical Literacy & Persuasion" unit and the final "Persuasive Campaign" project.

  • Distribute and read through the Unit Project Brief as a class.
  • Clarify the three components: Speech, Visual Text, and Rationale.
  • Introduce the PIE framework (Persuade, Inform, Entertain) using the Author's Purpose Handout. Explain that while all texts have a primary purpose, they often contain elements of the others.

3. Project Work: Campaign Brainstorm (45 mins)

Students are placed into their project groups (3-4 students).

  • Task 1 (25 mins): In groups, students complete the "PIE Practice" section of the Author's Purpose handout to solidify their understanding. Teacher circulates to check for understanding.
  • Task 2 (20 mins): Groups begin brainstorming potential topics for their campaign project. They should aim for a list of 3-5 social or environmental issues they are passionate about. Encourage them to think about issues relevant to their school, community, or country.

4. Plenary / Exit Ticket (5 mins)

Each student writes on a sticky note one issue they are most excited to work on for their campaign and sticks it on the board on their way out. This gives the teacher a quick overview of popular topics.
