Lesson 10: Campaign Presentations

Showcasing and evaluating the persuasive campaigns.

Learning Intentions (Ngā Whāinga Ako)

  • To deliver a polished and fluent persuasive speech (whaikōrero) as part of a group.
  • To present a cohesive campaign that integrates a speech and a visual text.
  • To critically and respectfully evaluate the work of peers as a responsible audience member.

NZC Links (Level 5)

  • **Speaking, Writing, and Presenting:** "Deliver a fluent and coherent oral text that is organised and structured for a particular purpose, and justifies the choices made."
  • **Listening, Reading, and Viewing:** "Analyse and evaluate how verbal and non-verbal language is used to create meaning and effect in a range of oral, written, and visual texts."

Cultural Competencies (Tātaiako)

  • Manaakitanga: Creating a supportive and respectful environment for presenters by being an attentive and encouraging audience.
  • Tangata Whenuatanga: Affirming the mana and identity of presenters by listening respectfully to their ideas and passions.

Resources

Assessment Rubric

Completed student campaigns (speeches, visuals, rationales).

Lesson Activities (75 Minutes)

Note: These presentations may take place over several lessons, depending on class size. This structure is for one 75-minute block.

1. Whakatau / Setting the Stage (5 mins)

Recite a karakia or whakataukī to open the space for presentations, e.g., "Mā te kōrero, ka mōhio; mā te mōhio, ka mārama." (Through discussion comes knowledge; through knowledge comes understanding.) Remind students of their role as a supportive audience (manaakitanga).

2. Presentations / Tūmahi (60 mins)

Each group delivers their persuasive campaign to the class. The structure for each presentation should be:

  • Briefly introduce their topic (kaupapa) and campaign goal.
  • Present their visual text (show poster, play video, etc.).
  • Deliver their 2-3 minute persuasive speech (whaikōrero).

Audience Task: While other groups are presenting, students use the "Two Stars and a Wish" model to provide written feedback for the presenting group. This will be collected and passed on at the end of all presentations.

  • Star 1: A specific strength of the speech.
  • Star 2: A specific strength of the visual.
  • Wish: A constructive suggestion for the campaign overall.

3. Q&A / Wānanga (5 mins per group)

After each presentation, the teacher facilitates a brief Q&A session where the audience can ask clarifying questions. The teacher provides summative feedback based on the assessment rubric.

4. Whakakapi / Unit Reflection (5 mins)

As a final reflection on the unit, students complete the following sentence in their books: "The most important thing I have learned about using my voice to create change is..."
