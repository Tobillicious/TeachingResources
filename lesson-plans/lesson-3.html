Lesson 3: The Art of Persuasion

Using Rhetorical Devices: Ethos, Pathos, and Logos.

Learning Intentions (Ngā Whāinga Ako)

  • To define and identify the three artistic proofs: Ethos, Pathos, and Logos.
  • To analyse how a speaker uses these devices to create a persuasive effect.
  • To apply these devices to strengthen their own persuasive writing.

NZC Links (Level 5)

  • **Listening, Reading, and Viewing:** "Analyse how language features are used to create meaning and effect in a range of texts."
  • **Speaking, Writing, and Presenting:** "Select, develop, and communicate ideas on a range of topics, making deliberate language choices to create an impact."

Cultural Competencies (Tātaiako)

  • Wānanga: Engaging in robust dialogue and analysis of how different persuasive techniques work.
  • Tangata Whenuatanga: Affirming student identity by allowing them to analyse texts and ads that are relevant to their own lives and cultures.

Resources

Writer's Toolkit: Rhetorical Devices Speech Analysis Handout

Lesson Activities (75 Minutes)

1. Whakatau / Do Now: The Appeal (10 mins)

Provide groups with a small selection of short written or visual texts (e.g., a print ad, a tweet, a charity appeal). In groups, students sort them based on their primary appeal: Are they appealing to emotion, to logic/reason, or to trust/character? Groups share back their categories and reasoning.

2. Explicit Teaching: The Mauri of Persuasion (20 mins)

Teacher connects the "Do Now" to the three artistic proofs (Ethos, Pathos, Logos), explaining these are the ancient Greek terms for the concepts students have just identified. They are the *mauri* (life force) of persuasive language.

  • Distribute the Rhetorical Devices handout. Explain each concept, linking back to the examples from the Do Now.
  • As a class, complete the deconstruction activity (Question 1) on the handout.
  • Briefly introduce the Speech Analysis Handout.

3. Project Work: Weaving in Ngā Toi (The Arts of Persuasion) (40 mins)

Students get into their campaign project groups with their PEEL paragraphs from the previous lesson.

  • Task 1 (20 mins): Groups analyse the "Empty Playground" speech excerpt on the Speech Analysis handout, identifying examples of Ethos, Pathos, and Logos.
  • Task 2 (20 mins): Groups take their own PEEL paragraphs and work to enhance them by deliberately weaving in at least one example of Ethos, Pathos, or Logos. They should aim to make their arguments more compelling.

Differentiation:

  • Support: Provide a cloze activity where students insert appropriate rhetorical appeals into a pre-written paragraph.
  • Extension: Challenge students to find a new example of Ethos, Pathos, or Logos in a real-world text (e.g., a news article, a blog post, a social media campaign) and explain its effect.

4. Whakakapi / Exit Ticket (5 mins)

Each group shares one "before" and "after" sentence, explaining how their addition of a rhetorical device made their argument stronger.
