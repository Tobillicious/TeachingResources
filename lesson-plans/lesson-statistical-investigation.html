Lesson: Statistical Investigation

Plan and conduct investigations using the statistical enquiry cycle.

Learning Objectives

  • Ākonga will be able to identify the steps of the statistical enquiry cycle.
  • Ākonga will be able to formulate a question for a statistical investigation.
  • Ākonga will be able to plan a simple data collection method.

Materials

  • Copies of the "Statistical Investigation Guide" handout
  • Whiteboard or large sheets of paper
  • Markers, pens, and pencils

Lesson Sequence

1. Introduction (10 mins)

Start with a question: "How could we find out the most popular lunch food at our school?" Discuss initial ideas and introduce the idea of a systematic investigation.

2. The Enquiry Cycle (15 mins)

Introduce the PPDAC (Problem, Plan, Data, Analysis, Conclusion) cycle using the handout. Use the "School Lunch Investigation" example to illustrate each step.

3. Planning an Investigation (25 mins)

In pairs, students choose a topic and work through the "Plan Your Own Investigation" section of the handout. They should focus on writing a clear question and planning their data collection.

4. Sharing Plans (10 mins)

Each pair shares their investigation question and their chosen data collection method. The class can offer feedback on the clarity and feasibility of the plans.

Assessment for Learning

Review the "Plan Your Own Investigation" section of the handouts. Are the questions clear? Are the data collection methods appropriate? Use this as a formative assessment to guide the next steps of the investigation.

Differentiation

For Support: Provide a list of pre-defined investigation questions for students to choose from. Work with a small group to co-construct a plan.

For Extension: Challenge students to think about potential biases in their data collection plan. How could they make their data more reliable?
